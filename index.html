01

Complete Build · 7 Months · Personal Project

1987 Mazda RX-7 LS Swap

End-to-end build converting an original 13B rotary RX-7 to a 6.0L GM LQ9 V8 paired with an AR5 5-speed manual. Required custom-fabricated mounts, full ECU re-tuning, and dyno validation.

400 WHP on Dyno
~$15K Cost Savings
7 mo Complete Build

Technical Highlights

  • Custom mount fabrication. Engine and transmission mounts designed and fabricated from scratch to position the LQ9 optimally within the RX-7 engine bay.
  • ECU re-calibration in TunerPro. Re-mapped the factory truck calibration to work with an aftermarket camshaft and valve springs, refining fuel and ignition tables iteratively through road and dyno testing.
  • Full systems integration. Adapted cooling, fuel, exhaust, electrical, and driveline subsystems into a chassis never engineered for the powertrain.
  • Cost engineering. Sourced junkyard cores and performed all labour myself to deliver an estimated $15,000 in savings vs. shop-built quotes.
Fabrication ECU Tuning TunerPro Systems Integration Dyno Testing
02

Mechatronic Project · MATLAB & Arduino

Penguingineer Adventures

An original 2D video game built from the ground up in MATLAB, combining a custom real-time physics engine with an Arduino-based hardware controller. Every subsystem including the solver and rendering was implemented from scratch.

Technical Highlights

  • RK4 physics solver. Implemented a fourth-order Runge–Kutta integrator to numerically solve the ODEs governing in-game motion, chosen over simpler schemes for its stability at interactive frame rates.
  • Arduino handshake protocol. Built a custom serial communication scheme between MATLAB and an Arduino microcontroller, ensuring synchronized, low-latency input from the physical controller.
  • Custom controller hardware. Designed and wired the input circuit from schematic to breadboard, mapping physical buttons to Arduino digital pins.
  • Modular code architecture. Cleanly separated physics, input, rendering, and game-state logic for testability and extensibility.
MATLAB Arduino Numerical Methods Embedded Mechatronics
03

Dynamics · Group Design Project · Dalhousie

Linkage-Driven Competition Vehicle

A LEGO-based competition vehicle propelled entirely by linkages, engineered to perform across both a 3 m speed trial and a five-stage obstacle course.

6.16s Speed Trial
3 Gear Ratios
52°+ Min. Trans. Angle

Technical Highlights

  • Four-bar linkage drive. Synthesized and analyzed in Linkage software; verified Grashof condition and minimum transmission angles of 52° (front) and 57° (rear) against a 45° design threshold.
  • Three-speed variable transmission. 0.36:1 / 1:1 / 2.78:1 gear ratios let the same vehicle compete in both a high-speed trial and a high-torque obstacle course.
  • Iterative design. First iteration ("the Centipede") used crank-sliders that failed to produce upward force; the four-bar coupler-path design replaced it after kinematic analysis.
  • Post-test failure analysis. Identified rear brake geometry and increased structural rigidity as concrete next-iteration improvements.
Machine Design Kinematics Linkage Software Drivetrain
